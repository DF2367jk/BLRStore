How come there is so much money available to help with retirement?

Depending on what you studied in school they may or may not have explained the rule of 72 !

At 7.2% interest, your money will double every 10 years. The index we used for your illustration does not allow you to participate in negative years. At 10% interest, your money will double every 7.2 years.

Apply this to a newborn. If they retire at 72, you did not have to fund them with a great deal of money as it will double many times.

Apply this to a 10-year-old. You fund it with the same amount over the same number of years and they receive half a much. The younger they are when they start makes a big difference.

This is why we receive much less or need to fund with much more when we wait until we are 40 to start a plan like this. Also, when you are older the amount of money needed to fund the life insurance portion is much more expensive.
